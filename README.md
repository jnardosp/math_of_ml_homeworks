# Math of ML homeworks
A repo for all homeworks of "Matemáticas del aprendizaje de máquina" course in Universidad Nacional de Colombia (UNAL).

## Homework 1: Convex Optimization related to Hard Margin SVMs
We explore the **convex optimization problem** related to **Support Vector Machines (SVMs)**, covering the mathematical problem setup, **convexity proofs**, **Lagrange multipliers**, **KKT conditions**, and the transition from **primal to dual** optimization problems for SVM training.

## Homework 2: Perceptron Learning
We explore the **perceptron** classical model, how it learns and why it works.

## Homework 3: Entropy formula explanation 
Because **entropy** is used in **tree-based models** with **ID3 algorithm**, we will explore why the entropy of a random variable is formulated like that.

## Homework 4: Netflix challenge & non-negative matrix factorization
We explore the **2007 Netflix Prize competition**, focusing on predicting movie rating behavior to improve recommendation systems. The task involves *predicting whether users rated movies* in 2006 based on a dataset of **100,000 user/movie pairs**, with the evaluation metric being **RMSE** (Root Mean Squared Error).

The solution examines the winning approach described in the "*KDD Cup 2007 Task 1 Winner Report*" co-authored by ****Miklós Kurucz**, specifically analyzing how their methods *relate to Positive Matrix Factorization (PMF) techniques*.

Key aspects covered:
- Prediction of movie rating probabilities for user/movie pairs
- The challenge of estimating ratings for pairs not present in the training set
- Relationship between the winning methods and non-negative matrix factorization approaches
